Argument from Existence
Vancouver's voluntary, density-bonus approach to inclusionary zoning produces fewer affordable units, at a lower percentage of new supply, with a higher opt-out rate than any of the three comparison jurisdictions. Portland's mandatory program produces 50% more units per year. Vienna's "Widmungskategorie" system delivers three times the proportion of affordable units in new development, with zero in-lieu substitutions. The common factor in higher-performing systems is mandatory requirements with no cash-out option. The argument that mandatory inclusionary zoning is infeasible does not survive contact with Portland's decade of implementation data, let alone Vienna's century-long social housing tradition. The tools exist. The question is whether Vancouver chooses to use them.
