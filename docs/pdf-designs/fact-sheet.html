City Approved 14 Variances Without Public Notice in 2025

14 variances approved without public notice since January 2025

Between January and October 2025, the City of Maplewood approved 14 zoning variances through its consent agenda process, bypassing the public notification requirements set out in Bylaw 4102-2019. None of the affected property owners within the 100-metre notification radius received mailed notice.

Key Findings
  1. All 14 variances were processed through the consent agenda rather than appearing as individual items on the Development Permits Committee agenda. Consent agenda items receive no individual debate and are approved in a single motion. [1]
  2. Bylaw 4102-2019, s. 12(3) requires mailed notice to all property owners within 100 metres of a proposed variance at least 14 days before the hearing date. FOI records confirm no notices were sent for any of the 14 applications. [2]
  3. Eleven of the 14 variances involved properties owned by three development firms — Greenway Developments Inc., Pinnacle Urban Corp., and Bridgewood Holdings Ltd. — all of which made donations to at least one sitting councillor's 2022 campaign. [3]
  4. The estimated assessed value increase across the 14 properties totals $8.2 million, based on BC Assessment data for comparable variance-adjusted parcels in adjacent municipalities. [4]
  5. Two of the variances reduced setback requirements below the fire separation minimums referenced in the BC Building Code (Article 9.10.14.1), raising questions about whether building permits should have been issued. [5]
