City Council — Rezoning Hearing — Block 14
May 12, 2026 Council Chambers, 7:00 PM
The Ask
Defer the Block 14 rezoning vote until the traffic impact study is completed and made available for public review.
Talking Points
  1. The traffic impact study was required by Council's own motion and has not been delivered.
    On November 18, 2024, Council passed Motion 2024-RC-112 requiring a traffic impact assessment before any rezoning of Block 14 could proceed. As of April 2026, the study has not been completed or made public. Proceeding without it contradicts Council's own condition of approval. Source: Council Minutes, Nov. 18, 2024, Motion 2024-RC-112 [1]
  2. The proposed density increase will generate 1,400 additional daily vehicle trips on roads already at 94% capacity.
    The applicant's own preliminary traffic memo estimates 1,400 new daily trips. The Ministry of Transportation's 2024 corridor assessment rated the adjacent section of Highway 7 at 94% capacity during evening peak. Adding this volume without mitigation infrastructure risks Level of Service F conditions. Source: Greenway Developments Traffic Memo, Aug. 2025 [2]; MoTI Corridor Assessment 2024 [3]
  3. Comparable rezonings in adjacent municipalities required completed impact studies before public hearing.
    Both the District of Lakeview (Bylaw 5501, Block 7, 2023) and the City of Ridgemont (Bylaw 2023-041, Sector 12, 2024) required completed traffic and environmental impact studies as conditions precedent to scheduling a public hearing. Maplewood's process for Block 14 falls below the procedural standard set by neighbouring jurisdictions. Source: District of Lakeview Council Minutes, Mar. 2023 [4]; City of Ridgemont staff report, Jan. 2024 [5]
Anticipated Pushback
What they'll say
Your response
"The traffic study is underway. We can approve in principle and finalize the study before building permits are issued."
Motion 2024-RC-112 requires the study before rezoning, not before building permits. Approval in principle creates an expectation of approval that makes future refusal politically and legally difficult. The study needs to precede the decision, not follow it.
"We've been working on this for two years. Further delays cost the applicant money and slow down housing supply."
The delay is caused by the applicant's failure to deliver the study that Council required. The community should not bear the risk of an uninformed approval because the applicant missed a deliverable. Due process is not a cost to be minimized.
"The preliminary traffic memo shows impacts are manageable."
A preliminary memo from the applicant's own consultant is not a traffic impact study. It was not peer-reviewed, does not model peak-hour conditions at the Highway 7 interchange, and omits cumulative impacts from the three other developments approved in 2024. Council required a study, not a memo.
